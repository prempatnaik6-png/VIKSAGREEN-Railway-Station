SHEET 01 / 12 — OVERVIEW
What is VIKSAGREEN?

A railway station that generates its own power.

VIKSAGREEN is derived from Viksit Sankalp Green Railway Station — an idea for how a railway station can become self-sufficient in most of its electricity needs, without adding to fossil-fuel dependence or cost.

A station like New Delhi Railway Station consumes roughly 5 MW of continuous power just for non-traction auxiliary uses — lighting, fans, HVAC, and water pumps — costing around ₹1.5–1.75 crore every month. Almost all of that comes from fossil-fuel-based electricity. VIKSAGREEN replaces that dependence with a combination of renewable energy sources, each contributing a share of the load, so the installation pays for itself in operating-cost savings within a few months of continuous use.

CAPEX / OPEX

Upfront cost, fast payback

Installation cost is significant, but daily maintenance is low — early estimates suggest the system can begin cutting real costs within 2–3 months of operation.

SAVINGS ESTIMATE

₹20–35 lakh saved monthly

Replacing non-traction fossil-fuel electricity with combined renewables could cut a station's monthly bill by this range, or more with judicious use.

CORE IDEA

Many small sources, one system

No single renewable source produces enough on its own — VIKSAGREEN's approach is to combine several at once and let smart automation use each efficiently.

SHEET 02 / 12 — THE PROBLEM
The Problem

Railways run on old energy — and no one is watching the stations.

India's railways are constantly modernising trains — electric and hydrogen-driven engines, faster rolling stock — but station-level energy consumption has been left behind. Lighting, fans, HVAC, and water pumps at stations still draw heavily on fossil-fuel electricity, every single day, at scale.

Fact Check

Indian Railways is India's single largest electricity consumer, using over 20 billion kWh a year — roughly 2–2.4% of the nation's total power generation.

85% Traction
15%

Non-traction use — stations, offices, lighting, HVAC, water pumps — is the smaller 15% slice. It's exactly what VIKSAGREEN targets.

"Two percent of the nation's grid drives a hundred percent of our railway momentum."

Why focus on stations, not trains?

Traction — actually running the trains — already gets the innovation budget: electric and hydrogen engines, regenerative braking, and more. Stations don't. They sit still, draw power continuously, and are rarely designed around energy efficiency. That makes them the highest-leverage, most overlooked place to intervene — and exactly where VIKSAGREEN focuses.

SHEET 03 / 12 — OBJECTIVES
Aims & Objectives

What VIKSAGREEN sets out to do.

  • Build a self-sustainable green railway station powered by renewable energy.
  • Reduce non-traction electricity consumption and operational costs at Indian railway stations.
  • Integrate solar, wind, hydroelectric, piezoelectric, and vertical-axis wind energy into one working ecosystem.
  • Maximise renewable generation using solar tracking and efficient energy-recovery techniques.
  • Improve railway safety with an automatic obstacle-detection and braking system inspired by KAVACH.
  • Minimise electricity wastage with an intelligent lighting system based on daylight and train movement.
  • Support India's clean-energy mission by reducing dependence on fossil fuels in railway infrastructure.
SHEET 04 / 12 — CONCEPT
Concept & Design Philosophy

One idea drives every part of this model:

"Generate more, waste less, and make every railway station self-sustainable."

Rather than betting on one renewable source, VIKSAGREEN layers several together with intelligent automation, so the whole system generates more than any single technology could — and wastes less than a station running on fixed, always-on infrastructure.

SHEET 05 / 12 — ENERGY SYSTEMS
The Solution

Five renewable systems, working together.

01 — SOLAR

Rotating Solar Tracking Panels

Two LDR sensors per panel feed an Arduino Uno, which drives a servo motor to tilt the panel toward the strongest light. A rotating panel captures noticeably more energy across the day than a static one.

Build: Arduino Uno · 2× LDR per panel · servo motor · LED indicator.

Exhibition note: run from a 9V battery or laptop, since indoor lighting is limited.
02 — WIND

Wind Turbines & Roadside VAWTs

Conventional wind turbines near the station, plus vertical-axis wind turbines beside access roads that spin from the air disturbance of passing vehicles.

Exhibition note: rotated by motor for demonstration — indoor wind is insufficient; the real-world mechanism is passive rotation.
03 — HYDRO

Micro-Hydroelectricity from Rainwater

Harvested rainwater is directed through a tube to spin a turbine. A secondary loop uses a solar-powered pump to recycle water back to the turbine, so generation continues between rainfalls.

Exhibition note: pump powered by 9V battery in place of a solar panel, due to limited indoor sunlight.
04 — PIEZOELECTRIC

Footstep Power on Platforms

6–8 piezoelectric plates installed under platform flooring generate a small charge per footstep — multiplied across thousands of daily passengers, it becomes meaningful. An Arduino and LCD display the energy generated in real time.

05 — SPEED BREAKER

Rotating Speed Breakers

Outside the station, speed breakers connected to a DC motor generate a small charge every time a vehicle passes over them — an alternative or complement to piezoelectric flooring.

SHEET 06 / 12 — SAFETY & EFFICIENCY
Smart Systems

Saving energy isn't just about generating more.

SAFETY

Automatic Obstacle Detection & Braking

Inspired by India's KAVACH system. A long-range sensor detects distant obstacles and sounds a buzzer to alert the loco pilot; if the train gets too close, a short-range sensor triggers automatic braking. Early warnings let the driver slow down gradually — which itself uses less energy than a hard automatic stop.

EFFICIENCY

Adaptive Lighting

IR and LDR sensors track ambient daylight and train arrivals. Lights sit at a baseline 40–60% intensity and rise to 80–100% only when daylight drops or a train is arriving — cutting wasted lighting energy through the day.

SHEET 07 / 12 — PHASE 2
Future Scope — In Development

What comes after this prototype.

These ideas are part of the VIKSAGREEN vision but are being held back for a regional/national-level version of the project.

  • CO₂-absorbing algae walls to improve station air quality, doubling as a biomass energy source.
  • Waste-to-hydrogen plants producing green hydrogen for engine fuel.
  • Track-bed sand geothermal harvesting — sand spread along the track absorbs solar heat by day and train-induced vibration keeps it in motion, and this stored heat could be tapped as a low-grade geothermal energy source.
  • Bio-toilets paired with the algae-wall system to convert human waste into biogas/biomass energy, with algae further treating the effluent.
  • Centralised power storage with rectifiers and capacitors for all five energy sources.
  • Face recognition / QR / smart-card entry (ESP32-based) to manage crowding and conserve resources.
  • AI-based energy optimisation and an IoT monitoring dashboard.
  • Predictive maintenance and smart-grid integration.
  • EV charging stations and battery energy storage systems.
  • Expansion toward net-zero stations, metro stations, and airports.
Energy note: heated track-bed sand and bio-toilet biomass are early concepts noted alongside the static CO₂-absorbing wall model — not yet built as working circuits in this prototype, but flagged here as directions for the next phase.
